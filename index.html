FAQ

You have questions. We have answers.

What exactly is a Captive?
A captive is simply an insurance company created and owned by the people it insures. Instead of buying insurance from a big corporation, you and your association peers form your own "mini" insurance company to cover your own businesses.
Is this legal and regulated?
Yes. Group captives are heavily regulated and must adhere to strict state or offshore regulatory requirements to ensure solvency, exactly like traditional insurance carriers. Captive8 handles all of this compliance for you.
What happens if there's a huge catastrophic claim?
Your group's captive pool is designed to handle common, predictable claims. For large, catastrophic events, the captive purchases "reinsurance" (insurance for the insurance company) to absorb the massive hits and protect the members.
Do my members have to leave their current broker to join?
Not at all. We regularly work alongside members' existing retail brokers. We view brokers as partners and allow them to continue advising your members while utilizing our captive programs.
Is my association large enough to do this?
Captives used to be exclusively for fortune 500 companies. With Captive8's technology platform, we can launch efficient, profitable syndicate groups for associations of almost any size. The easiest way to find out is to request a free savings analysis.
